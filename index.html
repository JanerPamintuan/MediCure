Health Tips
Simple habits that make a big difference to your wellbeing for PSAU Community.
01

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses (2 liters) of water daily. Proper hydration supports kidney function, improves energy, and keeps your skin healthy. Carry a reusable water bottle with you on campus.

02

Take Medicines Correctly

Always follow the prescribed dosage and timing. Never skip doses of antibiotics even if you feel better. Store medicines in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

03

Exercise Regularly

Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate physical activity most days. Walking, jogging, or stretching between classes all count. Regular exercise reduces stress and boosts immunity.

04

Get Proper Sleep

Students need 7–9 hours of sleep per night. Poor sleep weakens your immune system and impairs concentration. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends.

05

Eat Balanced Meals

Fill half your plate with vegetables and fruits, a quarter with lean protein, and a quarter with whole grains. Limit ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive salt.

06

Practice Good Hygiene

Wash hands with soap for at least 20 seconds before eating and after using the restroom. Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing to prevent spreading illness on campus.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions about the clinic and your health.

What should I do in an emergency?

Quick Help
Q: I feel very ill or injured on campus. What do I do?

Go directly to the PSAU Infirmary or call 0912-345-6789. For life threatening emergencies, call the national emergency line (911). Inform your teacher or a nearby staff member immediately.

Q: Does the clinic handle dental emergencies?

The clinic handles basic first aid for dental pain, but also do dental care, if it really needed an emergency students are referred to an external dentist.

Proper Use of Medicine

Medicine Use
Q: Can I get medicines from the clinic for free?

Yes, the infirmary provides basic medicines to enrolled PSAU students and staff at no charge, subject to availability.

Q: Can I take leftover antibiotics for a new illness?

No. Always consult a doctor before taking antibiotics. Using the wrong antibiotic or not finishing a full course can make bacteria resistant to treatment.

Medicine Warnings

Warnings
Q: What are common medicine side effects to watch for?

Watch for rashes, difficulty breathing, severe stomach pain, or unusual swelling. These may indicate an allergic reaction — stop the medicine and seek help immediately.

Q: Is it safe to mix over-the-counter medicines?

Not always. Some combinations (e.g., Ibuprofen + Mefenamic Acid) can increase the risk of stomach bleeding. Always consult the clinic before combining medicines.

When To Seek Medical Help?

Seek Help
Q: My fever has lasted 3 days. Should I see a doctor?

Yes. A fever lasting more than 3 days, or one that is 38.5°C or higher, warrants a medical consultation. It could be a sign of infection requiring proper treatment.

Q: I've had diarrhea for more than a day. What should I do?

Visit the clinic if diarrhea lasts more than 24 hours, is accompanied by blood, or if you feel very weak. Stay hydrated with water or oral rehydration salts (ORS) in the meantime.
